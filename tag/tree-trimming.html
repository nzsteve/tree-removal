Techniques Used in Tree Trimming

Learning about various techniques used during tree trimming in Chiswick can empower you as a homeowner. Here are a few common methods that professionals deploy:

Crown Thinning

This method involves selectively removing branches throughout the crown (the upper part) of the tree to allow light penetration while reducing wind resistance. It promotes healthy growth without altering natural shape.

Crown Reduction

Ideal for overgrown trees nearing structures or interfering with power lines, crown reduction carefully shortens branches to reduce height while maintaining aesthetics.

Deadwooding

As its name suggests, deadwooding entails removing dead or dying branches from living trees. This process mitigates risks associated with falling branches and enhances overall safety in residential areas.

Pollarding

A specialized technique typically used on older specimens; pollarding involves cutting back young shoots annually after they have reached a specific height which increases foliage thickness over time while retaining structure vitality.
